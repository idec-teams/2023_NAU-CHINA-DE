      2023/9/23

      Member: H.T.Q, Z.M.L, L.Y.X

      Experiment:

      Time response curve determination

      (1) Add positive bacterial solution into 96 deep well plate and culture until the OD600 of the bacterial solution reach 0.6

      (2) Add 1mM liquiritigenin into each well

      (3) Sample from 96 deep well plate each hour until 12h

      (4) 600μL bacterial solution was centrifuged at 5000 rpm for 3 min after the induction culture completed, and then discard the supernatant. 600 μL cold PBS buffer was pipetted and resuspended for 2 times, and 200μL was taken into a 96-well fluorescent plate.

      Additionally, PBS should be added either. (as bacground)

      (5) Samples were taken from the and fluorescence detected with a microplate reader (Ex=482nm/ Ex=524nm and Ex=478nm/ Ex=516nm)

      (6) Data analysis

      Result:

      As time goes on, the response of the mutant biosensor continues to enhance, with a stronger response compared to the wild type.

      1.Determination the naringenin concentration response curve of better mutants (1mM)

      Result:

      The curve shows that the response level of the mutant to naringenin is lower than that to liquiritigenin, and much lower than that of the wild type.
